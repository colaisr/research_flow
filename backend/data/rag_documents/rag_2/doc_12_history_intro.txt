Title: A Short Introduction to the History of Navigation

For thousands of years, humans have looked to the stars to find their way. Early sailors relied on the Sun, the Moon, and constellations to guide their travels across open seas. The invention of the magnetic compass in China during the Han dynasty marked the beginning of more reliable navigation. By the 15th century, European explorers used astrolabes and improved maps to cross oceans and connect continents, accelerating global trade and cultural exchange.
